Square Scale

How to use Square Scale

1
Search for a place. Type any country, US state, city, island, Japanese prefecture, or region into the search box and tap a result to add it to the canvas.
2
Each square represents area. The bigger the square, the bigger the place. All squares are to the same scale, so you can directly compare sizes at a glance.
3
Drag squares around. Tap and drag any square to move it. Tap a square to select it (black border).
4
Combine touching squares. Drag two or more squares so they overlap, select one, then tap Combine Touching. They merge into a single square showing the combined area — great for "how many Californias fit in Russia?"
5
Split them back apart. Select a combined square and tap De-combine to restore the originals.
6
Share your comparison. Tap Share to copy a link that recreates your exact layout for anyone you send it to.
💡 Tiny places (like Monaco or Singapore) are shown with an orange glow and a "NOT TO SCALE" badge — they're enlarged so you can see them, but aren't drawn to the same scale as larger regions.
💡 Cities appear in three versions: City Proper, Metro Area, and Urban Footprint — add all three to compare how differently each boundary is drawn.
